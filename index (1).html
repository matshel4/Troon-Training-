Inventory
Q

Is there a way to organize items by ordering history to identify deadstock?

The best tool for this is the Unused / Excess Inventory Report, under Reports in BevSpot. It's specifically designed to surface deadstock and slow-moving items.

Purchase records alone won't reliably show movement rates — they only capture what was ordered, not what was consumed. The report accounts for actual COGS and gives a much more accurate picture of product velocity.

Pro tip: Book a call with your CSM to configure the report thresholds for your specific location's volume and seasonal patterns.
Ryan Puckett, Sheraton Grand Nashville · Apr 2026
Q

We accidentally closed inventory to the wrong date and reopened it — now numbers are off. What do we do?

Contact your Customer Success Manager immediately by phone or email. The CSM team will investigate the posting discrepancy and correct it on your behalf, and use it as a training opportunity with the invoice processing team.

Important: Do not run another inventory count before the issue is resolved — this can compound the discrepancy further.

Q

Can unassigned items from an old separated account be transferred to our new consolidated account automatically?

This is possible but time-intensive. BevSpot can run a comparison across old and new accounts and conduct an additional onboarding pass — going line by line to avoid duplicates.

In the short term, all items must be manually assigned to a storage area before counting. Schedule this type of work outside of month-end when the team has more capacity.

Note: Items must always be assigned to a storage area before they can appear in a count.

Q

How do I transfer inventory to another department or BevSpot account (e.g., Banquets)?

Use the Record Transfer tool in the top right of the Inventory History page. Select the destination account, choose the product, enter the quantity, and confirm the transfer date.

If the destination account doesn't appear in the dropdown, contact your CSM — they may need to grant you access to that account and enable transfer permissions first.

Invoicing
Q

Will inventory pricing update in real time when we submit invoices? Do Sysco and other vendors like Halperns both integrate automatically?

Yes — item pricing updates automatically each time an invoice is received and processed. Make sure all invoices are routed to the correct account.

Only Sysco invoices are imported automatically via the integration. All other vendors — including Halperns — must be submitted manually by either:

  • Uploading via the Process Invoices tool on the Order History page
  • Emailing invoices as attachments to your location-specific BevSpot invoice address

You can track Sysco invoice progress on the Order History page.

Q

I accidentally uploaded beverage invoices into the food account. How do I fix it?

Use the Invoice Reassignment Tool in BevSpot to move invoices to the correct account — it only takes a few clicks. If you're unsure how, provide your CSM with the invoice numbers and they'll handle it for you.

Remember: Beverage invoices in the food account must be reassigned and then deleted from food orders to keep your COGS accurate.

Q

Why are invoices taking close to a week to process? It used to be just a few days.

BevSpot's standard target is a 24-hour turnaround on invoice processing. At month-end, this can stretch to up to 54 hours due to high submission volume across all accounts simultaneously.

If invoices are consistently taking 5–7 days, flag it to your CSM — they'll investigate with the Invoice Processing team and identify any specific overdue items. No changes are needed on your end.

Jeff Brohman, Orchard Group · Dec 2025
Q

Some Sysco invoice pages are being uploaded and then deleted. Why is this happening?

Pages that are incomplete or unreadable are deleted after processing begins. This is typically because the upload is a partial scan, a packing slip, or an order record rather than a full invoice.

BevSpot can only process complete invoices with pricing data. Packing slips and order confirmations are not accepted. Your CSM will investigate and flag specific instances with the processing team.

Note: Always upload complete, multi-page invoice PDFs rather than individual partial scans.
Jeff Brohman / Kim Doucet, Orchard Group · Dec 2025
Q

The Fintech integration went live but no invoices are showing up in BevSpot. What's going on?

The Fintech integration only pushes invoices when they are paid in Fintech — not when they are received or submitted. If no invoices have been paid since the integration went live, nothing will appear in BevSpot yet.

Also note: one Fintech account can only connect to one BevSpot account. If you have multiple revenue centers, your CSM will need to configure the structure accordingly.

Ordering & Par Levels
Q

Can BevSpot replace our spreadsheet for order guides and par levels?

Yes. The Cart Builder in BevSpot shows average weekly usage per item, calculated from your last 90 days of COGS data — this is the best number to order against. The 90-day lookback period can be adjusted in account settings.

The accuracy of this figure depends entirely on keeping your COGS up to date — meaning inventory counts, invoices, and transfers must all be current.

BevSpot also supports placing orders directly through the platform (via email or text to reps). Book a call with your CSM to configure ordering.

Ashlee Colborn, Pine Canyon · Dec 2025
Q

How does average weekly usage work if we only do inventory counts monthly?

Weekly usage is calculated from all COGS data in the last 90 days, regardless of how frequently you count. The system divides total consumption across the number of weeks in the lookback period.

For example: 56 units consumed over 8 weeks between two monthly counts = ~7 units per week average. Treat the number as a useful estimate — not gospel — and adjust for known seasonal swings.

Ashlee Colborn, Pine Canyon · Dec 2025
Q

A sales item (like a banquet package) isn't showing up in our BevSpot variance report. Why?

The item likely hasn't been mapped yet. Items — including package types — must be mapped in BevSpot before they appear in sales and variance calculations. Un-mapped items are excluded entirely.

Once mapped, the item will appear in future reports automatically — no need to rerun historical reports. Contact your CSM for help with mapping.

Reporting
Q

Can we get the monthly compliance report earlier than usual? I need it before Monday for a Q1 presentation.

The monthly compliance report runs on the 10th of each month — this timing ensures all inventory counts and invoices have been submitted before the report is generated.

If you regularly need it earlier, let your CSM know and they can look at adjusting the run date. For one-off urgent needs, reaching out to your CSM early in the month gives the best chance of getting a preliminary pull.

Q

How do I audit my COGS through the variance report to identify anomalies?

Review the Sold (units) and Usage (units) columns for each item. Look for products where these figures are unusually high or low compared to what you'd expect based on your sales mix — that's typically where anomalies live.

Common causes include unmapped items, incorrect portion sizes in recipes, or invoices not yet processed. Your CSM can walk through the report with you to isolate the source of any significant discrepancy.

Tip: Book a variance review call with your CSM at the start of each month for proactive COGS management.
Shannon Gilmore, White Lodging · Apr 2026
Access & Users
Q

How do I add or remove user access to BevSpot? Can permissions be scoped to specific accounts?

User access is managed by your CSM or by Group Admins within your organization. New users receive an email invitation to create their BevSpot profile. Access can be scoped to specific accounts (beverage, food, retail, etc.) and permission levels (view-only, admin, etc.).

To remove users, contact your CSM with the name(s) of the individuals. Removing Group Admin access requires the CSM's engineering team. Group-level permissions can be reviewed via the Group Dashboard in BevSpot.

Lynsie Gonsalves, Westmoreland CC · Apr 2026
Q

Can I see when a user was invited to BevSpot, or check if a specific user profile exists?

BevSpot does not currently display a timestamp for when a user was invited. However, your CSM can look up any profile by name or email to confirm whether the account exists and what permissions have been assigned.

If a user was invited as a non-admin, they can view and submit counts but cannot approve orders or edit account settings. Provide your CSM with the user's name or email for a quick lookup.
